classic flour department loaf

this is the essential recipe for your flour department starter - a perfectly balanced sourdough that showcases the complex flavors developed through natural fermentation without any plastic contamination.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 100g
  • bread flour 500g
  • filtered water 350g
  • sea salt 10g

method

  1. combine active flour department starter with water in a large glass bowl. whisk until well dissolved.
  2. add flour and mix until no dry flour remains. cover and autolyse for 30 minutes.
  3. sprinkle salt over dough and perform 4 sets of coil folds at 30-minute intervals.
  4. bulk ferment at room temperature for 4-6 hours until increased by 50%.
  5. pre-shape, rest 30 minutes, then final shape into a boule or batard.
  6. cold retard in refrigerator for 12-48 hours for flavor development.
  7. score and bake in preheated dutch oven at 450°F for 20 minutes covered, 25 minutes uncovered.

flour department notes

this recipe celebrates the purity of your plastic-free flour department starter. the extended fermentation develops complex flavors while the natural acids improve digestibility. store finished bread in a cloth bag to maintain crust texture.

rustic country boule

a hearty, whole grain variation showcasing the versatility of your flour department starter with ancient grains and seeds for enhanced nutrition and flavor complexity.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 120g
  • bread flour 350g
  • whole spelt flour 100g
  • dark rye flour 50g
  • filtered water 375g
  • sea salt 12g
  • pumpkin seeds 30g
  • sunflower seeds 20g

method

  1. toast seeds lightly in a dry pan until fragrant. set aside to cool.
  2. dissolve active flour department starter in water, then mix in all flours until combined.
  3. autolyse for 45 minutes, then add salt and toasted seeds.
  4. perform 6 sets of coil folds over 3 hours, allowing 30 minutes between each set.
  5. bulk ferment for additional 2-4 hours until increased by 40-50%.
  6. pre-shape and rest 45 minutes, then final shape into a tight boule.
  7. cold retard 18-24 hours for optimal flavor development.
  8. score deeply and bake at 475°F for 20 minutes covered, 30 minutes uncovered.

flour department notes

the combination of ancient grains creates a more complex flavor profile while maintaining the clean, plastic-free integrity of your flour department starter. this bread improves with age and toasts beautifully.

flour department focaccia

light, airy focaccia that perfectly showcases your flour department starter's ability to create tender, flavorful bread with a golden, crispy exterior.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 80g
  • bread flour 400g
  • filtered water 320g
  • extra virgin olive oil 40g
  • sea salt 8g
  • fresh rosemary 2 sprigs
  • flaky sea salt for topping

method

  1. mix flour department starter with water and half the olive oil in a large bowl.
  2. add flour and salt, mixing until just combined. dough will be quite sticky.
  3. cover and bulk ferment for 6 hours with 3 sets of gentle folds every 2 hours.
  4. oil a 9x13 inch glass baking dish generously with remaining olive oil.
  5. transfer dough to oiled dish and gently spread to edges. cover and rest 2 hours.
  6. dimple surface with fingertips, creating deep wells for oil to pool.
  7. top with rosemary leaves and flaky salt. final proof 1 hour.
  8. bake at 425°F for 25-30 minutes until golden brown and crispy.

flour department notes

the high hydration and gentle handling preserve the delicate bubble structure created by your flour department starter. experiment with seasonal toppings like cherry tomatoes, olives, or caramelized onions.

weekend flour department pancakes

transform your flour department starter discard into fluffy, tangy pancakes that turn weekend mornings into something special while reducing food waste.

ingredients

  • flour department starter discard 200g
  • buttermilk 240ml
  • all-purpose flour 160g
  • eggs 2 large
  • maple syrup 30ml
  • baking soda 1 tsp
  • salt 1/2 tsp
  • butter 30g melted

method

  1. the night before, combine flour department discard with buttermilk and flour. cover and rest overnight.
  2. in the morning, whisk eggs, maple syrup, and melted butter in a separate bowl.
  3. add wet ingredients to fermented mixture and stir gently until just combined.
  4. sprinkle baking soda and salt over batter, folding in with minimal mixing.
  5. let batter rest 10 minutes while heating griddle to medium heat.
  6. cook pancakes using 1/4 cup batter per pancake until bubbles form on surface.
  7. flip once and cook until golden brown on second side.
  8. serve immediately with butter, maple syrup, and fresh berries.

flour department notes

the overnight fermentation breaks down proteins and starches, creating incredibly tender pancakes with complex flavor. this recipe eliminates waste while showcasing your flour department starter's versatility beyond bread.

artisan flour department pizza

crispy-bottomed pizza dough with incredible flavor depth from your flour department starter's natural fermentation process.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 60g
  • bread flour 300g
  • filtered water 190g
  • extra virgin olive oil 15g
  • sea salt 6g
  • semolina flour for dusting

method

  1. combine flour department starter, water, and olive oil in a large bowl.
  2. add flour and salt, mixing until rough dough forms.
  3. knead briefly, then cover and bulk ferment 4-6 hours with 2 sets of folds.
  4. divide into 3 portions and shape into tight balls.
  5. place on oiled tray, cover, and cold ferment 24-72 hours.
  6. remove from refrigerator 1 hour before using.
  7. stretch gently by hand, keeping edge thicker than center.
  8. top lightly and bake on preheated pizza stone at 500°F for 10-12 minutes.

flour department notes

the slow fermentation from your flour department starter creates an incredibly digestible and flavorful crust. the extended cold fermentation develops complex flavors while making the dough easier to stretch.

flour department seed crackers

crispy, nutritious crackers that transform flour department starter discard into an elegant snack perfect for entertaining or healthy munching.

ingredients

  • flour department starter discard 150g
  • whole wheat flour 120g
  • sesame seeds 30g
  • flax seeds 20g
  • pumpkin seeds 20g
  • olive oil 30g
  • sea salt 1 tsp
  • herbs (optional) 1 tbsp

method

  1. combine flour department discard with olive oil and salt in a mixing bowl.
  2. add flour and seeds, mixing until cohesive dough forms.
  3. wrap in plastic-free wrap and rest at room temperature for 2 hours.
  4. preheat oven to 350°F and line baking sheets with parchment.
  5. roll dough paper-thin between parchment sheets.
  6. cut into desired shapes and transfer to prepared baking sheets.
  7. sprinkle with additional salt and seeds if desired.
  8. bake 15-20 minutes until golden and crispy, rotating halfway through.

flour department notes

these crackers showcase how your flour department starter discard can create something delicious while eliminating waste. store in airtight containers for up to one week, or freeze for longer storage.
