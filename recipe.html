classic flour department loaf

the foundational recipe that showcases your flour department starter at its finest. this classic sourdough develops complex flavors through long fermentation.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 100g
  • bread flour 500g
  • water 375g
  • salt 10g

method

  1. mix starter with water until dissolved, then add flour and mix until no dry flour remains.
  2. autolyse for 30 minutes, then add salt and mix thoroughly.
  3. bulk ferment 4-6 hours with 4 sets of folds every 30 minutes for first 2 hours.
  4. pre-shape into round, rest 20 minutes, then final shape and place in banneton.
  5. cold proof 12-24 hours in refrigerator.
  6. preheat dutch oven to 475°F, score dough and bake covered 20 minutes.
  7. remove lid, reduce to 450°F and bake 20-25 minutes until golden.
  8. cool completely on wire rack before slicing.

flour department notes

timing is flexible - watch the dough, not the clock. proper fermentation creates the signature open crumb and complex flavor that makes this loaf special.

weekend flour department pancakes

fluffy, tangy pancakes that transform your starter discard into weekend magic. the overnight fermentation creates incredible depth of flavor.

ingredients

  • flour department starter discard 200g
  • buttermilk 240ml
  • all-purpose flour 100g
  • egg 1 large
  • melted butter 30g
  • sugar 1 tbsp
  • baking soda 1 tsp
  • salt 1/2 tsp

method

  1. night before, mix starter discard, buttermilk, and flour. cover and ferment overnight.
  2. in morning, whisk egg with melted butter and sugar until smooth.
  3. add egg mixture to fermented batter and stir gently.
  4. sprinkle baking soda and salt over batter, fold in until just combined.
  5. heat griddle to medium heat, lightly grease with butter.
  6. pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake, cook until bubbles form on surface.
  7. flip and cook 1-2 minutes more until golden brown.
  8. serve immediately with butter, syrup, and fresh berries.

flour department notes

don't overmix the final batter - lumps are perfectly fine. the overnight fermentation is key to developing that signature tangy flavor.

artisan flour department pizza

crispy-bottomed pizza with incredible flavor from slow fermentation. the flour department starter creates the perfect base for any topping combination.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 50g
  • bread flour 300g
  • water 200g
  • olive oil 2 tbsp
  • salt 6g
  • pizza sauce as needed
  • mozzarella cheese as needed
  • toppings of choice as desired

method

  1. mix starter with water and olive oil until well combined.
  2. add flour and salt, mix until shaggy dough forms.
  3. bulk ferment 4-6 hours with 3 sets of folds in first 2 hours.
  4. divide into 2-3 portions, shape into balls and rest 30 minutes.
  5. preheat pizza stone or steel to 500°F for at least 1 hour.
  6. stretch dough gently into pizza shape, avoiding overworking.
  7. top with sauce, cheese, and desired toppings.
  8. bake 10-12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

flour department notes

high heat is essential for crispy crust. let the dough come to room temperature before stretching for easier handling and better texture.

flour department focaccia

light, airy focaccia with a golden crust and dimpled surface perfect for olive oil pools. a showcase for seasonal toppings and herbs.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 100g
  • bread flour 400g
  • water 320g
  • olive oil 60ml
  • salt 8g
  • fresh rosemary 2 sprigs
  • coarse sea salt for topping

method

  1. mix starter with water and half the olive oil until smooth.
  2. add flour and salt, mix until no dry flour remains.
  3. bulk ferment 8-10 hours with 3 sets of folds in first 3 hours.
  4. oil a 9x13 inch pan generously, transfer dough to pan.
  5. gently stretch to fit pan, cover and proof 2-3 hours.
  6. dimple surface all over with oiled fingers, drizzle with remaining oil.
  7. top with rosemary and coarse salt.
  8. bake at 425°F for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.

flour department notes

the high hydration creates an incredibly light texture. generous oil and proper dimpling are key to authentic focaccia appearance and flavor.

crispy flour department waffles

golden, crispy waffles with perfectly tender interiors and deep pockets for syrup. the overnight fermentation creates incredible flavor depth.

ingredients

  • flour department starter discard 200g
  • buttermilk 300ml
  • all-purpose flour 150g
  • eggs 2 large
  • melted butter 60g
  • sugar 2 tbsp
  • baking soda 1 tsp
  • salt 1/2 tsp

method

  1. night before, mix starter discard, buttermilk, and flour. cover and ferment overnight.
  2. in morning, separate eggs. whisk yolks with melted butter and sugar.
  3. add egg mixture to fermented batter and stir gently until combined.
  4. whip egg whites to soft peaks.
  5. sprinkle baking soda and salt over batter, fold in gently.
  6. fold in whipped egg whites in two additions until just combined.
  7. cook in preheated waffle iron until golden and crispy.
  8. serve immediately with butter, syrup, and fresh berries.

flour department notes

the key to crispy waffles is not overmixing the batter and cooking until deeply golden. freeze extras for quick weekday breakfasts - just pop in the toaster.

everyday sandwich bread

soft, sliceable bread perfect for sandwiches and toast. enriched dough creates tender crumb while maintaining sourdough complexity.

ingredients

  • active flour department starter 100g
  • bread flour 450g
  • whole milk 240ml
  • butter, softened 30g
  • honey 2 tbsp
  • salt 8g
  • egg 1 large

method

  1. warm milk slightly, mix with starter and honey until combined.
  2. add flour, egg, salt, and softened butter. knead until smooth.
  3. bulk ferment 8-10 hours until doubled in oiled bowl.
  4. shape into loaf and place in greased 9x5 inch pan.
  5. cover and proof 3-4 hours until dough crowns the pan.
  6. brush top with beaten egg for golden crust.
  7. bake at 375°F for 35-40 minutes until golden brown.
  8. cool completely before slicing for clean cuts.

flour department notes

the enriched dough creates a tender, long-lasting loaf perfect for daily use. slice when completely cool for best texture and storage life.
